The Boy Who Lived

Harry Potter survives an attack by the dark wizard Voldemort, becoming famous as "The Boy Who Lived." His parents, Lily and James Potter, are killed protecting him.

The Dursleys

Harry grows up with his cruel relatives, the Dursleys: Uncle Vernon, Aunt Petunia, and their son Dudley. They keep Harry ignorant of his magical heritage.

Hogwarts Acceptance

On his eleventh birthday, Harry receives a letter from Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, delivered by Hagrid, the gentle giant gamekeeper of Hogwarts.

Diagon Alley

Harry visits Diagon Alley, the magical shopping street, with Hagrid. There, he buys his school supplies, including his wand from Mr. Ollivander's wand shop.

The Sorting Hat

Harry arrives at Hogwarts and is sorted into Gryffindor House during the Sorting Ceremony, alongside his new friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger.

The Philosopher's Stone

Harry learns about the Philosopher's Stone, which grants immortality, hidden within Hogwarts. Someone is trying to steal it, putting the school in danger.

The Forbidden Forest

During detention, Harry, Ron, and Hermione encounter a mysterious creature in the Forbidden Forest, leading them to discover clues about the theft of the Philosopher's Stone.

The Mirror of Erised

Harry finds the Mirror of Erised, which shows him his deepest desires. He sees his deceased parents in the mirror, providing both comfort and longing.

Quidditch

Harry joins the Gryffindor Quidditch team as a Seeker and plays in his first match against Slytherin, showcasing his natural talent on a broomstick.

The Final Confrontation

Harry, Ron, and Hermione uncover that Professor Quirrell is working for Voldemort and trying to steal the Philosopher's Stone. They confront him, leading to a battle where Harry prevents Voldemort from obtaining the stone and its powers.
